Sweet Desserts to Satisfy Your Cravings

Indulge in Irresistible Dessert Creations

Dive into a delectable universe of desserts, from classic favorites like Apple Pie to exotic treats like Bingsu etc.

You're a baking enthusiast or simply seeking the perfect dessert to satisfy your cravings, you'll find something here to tantalize your taste buds.

  • Apple Pie

    Preparation Time: 1 hour

    Ingredients:

    • 6-7 cups of thinly sliced and peeled apples (e.g., Granny Smith)
    • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
    • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
    • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
    • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
    • A pinch of salt
    • 2 tablespoons butter
    • 2 prepared pie crusts

    Recipe:

    1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
    2. In a large bowl, combine sliced apples, sugar, lemon juice, cinnamon, nutmeg, and salt. Mix well.
    3. Place one pie crust in a pie pan, and fill it with the apple mixture. Dot the top with butter.
    4. Cover with the second pie crust, seal the edges, and cut a few slits in the top crust to allow steam to escape.
    5. Bake in the preheated oven for 45 to 50 minutes until the pie is golden brown. Cool before serving.

  • Mochi

    Preparation Time: 2 hours

    Ingredients:

    • 1 cup glutinous rice flour
    • 1/2 cup sugar
    • 1 cup water
    • Cornstarch or potato starch (for dusting)
    • Desired fillings (e.g., red bean paste, ice cream)

    Recipe:

    1. Mix glutinous rice flour and sugar in a microwave-safe bowl. Stir in water until smooth.
    2. Cover and microwave for 1-2 minutes until the mixture becomes translucent, stirring once in between.
    3. Let it cool, then dust your hands and working surface with starch. Shape the mochi into small disks or squares.
    4. Add your favorite fillings, seal the edges, and dust with more starch to prevent sticking.
    5. Your delicious mochi is ready to enjoy!

  • Lemon Cheesecake

    Preparation Time: 3 hours

    Ingredients:

    • 1 1/2 cups graham cracker crumbs
    • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
    • 4 (8-ounce) packages cream cheese, softened
    • 1 1/4 cups granulated sugar
    • 4 large eggs
    • 1/3 cup fresh lemon juice
    • Zest of 2 lemons
    • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

    Recipe:

    1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C). Grease and line the base of a 9-inch springform pan.
    2. Mix graham cracker crumbs and melted butter, then press into the bottom of the prepared pan.
    3. In a large mixing bowl, beat cream cheese and sugar until smooth. Add eggs one at a time, mixing well after each addition.
    4. Stir in lemon juice, lemon zest, and vanilla extract. Pour the mixture over the crust.
    5. Bake for about 45-50 minutes until the edges are set but the center is slightly jiggly. Turn off the oven and let it cool in the oven with the door ajar.
    6. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 3 hours before serving.

  • Creme Brulee

    Preparation Time: 2 hours

    Ingredients:

    • 6 large egg yolks
    • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
    • 2 cups heavy cream
    • 1 tablespoon pure vanilla extract
    • 2 tablespoons light brown sugar

    Recipe:

    1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C). Place six ramekins in a large baking pan.
    2. Whisk together egg yolks and granulated sugar in a mixing bowl until well combined.
    3. In a saucepan, heat the heavy cream and vanilla extract over medium heat until it's about to boil.
    4. Slowly pour the hot cream into the egg yolk mixture, whisking constantly.
    5. Pour the custard into the ramekins. Fill the baking pan with boiling water to reach halfway up the sides of the ramekins.
    6. Bake in the preheated oven for about 45-50 minutes until the custard is set but still slightly wobbly in the center.
    7. Remove the ramekins from the water bath, let them cool, and refrigerate for a few hours or overnight.
    8. Sprinkle brown sugar on top of each custard and use a kitchen torch to caramelize the sugar until it's golden and crispy.

  • Tiramisu

    Preparation Time: 4 hours

    Ingredients:

    • 6 large egg yolks
    • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
    • 1 cup mascarpone cheese
    • 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
    • 1 cup brewed espresso, cooled
    • 1/4 cup coffee liqueur (e.g., Kahlúa)
    • 1 package (about 7 ounces) ladyfingers
    • Cocoa powder, for dusting
    • Dark chocolate shavings, for garnish

    Recipe:

    1. In a heatproof bowl, whisk together egg yolks and 1/2 cup of sugar until creamy and pale.
    2. Place the bowl over a pot of simmering water (double boiler) and whisk constantly for about 10 minutes until the mixture thickens. Remove from heat and let it cool.
    3. Add mascarpone cheese to the egg yolk mixture and stir until well combined.
    4. In a separate bowl, whip the heavy cream and the remaining 1/4 cup of sugar until stiff peaks form.
    5. Gently fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture until smooth.
    6. In a shallow dish, combine brewed espresso and coffee liqueur. Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the mixture and layer them in the bottom of a serving dish.
    7. Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers. Repeat with another layer of dipped ladyfingers and the remaining mascarpone mixture.
    8. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight.
    9. Before serving, dust the top with cocoa powder and garnish with chocolate shavings.

  • Bingsu

    Preparation Time: 20 minutes

    Ingredients:

    • Shaved ice
    • Condensed milk
    • Sweet toppings (e.g., red bean, fruit, ice cream)
    • Crispy cereal or rice cakes (optional)

    Recipe:

    1. Fill a bowl with shaved ice.
    2. Drizzle condensed milk generously over the ice.
    3. Add your choice of sweet toppings. Common options include red bean, fruit, and a scoop of ice cream.
    4. If you like some crunch, sprinkle crispy cereal or add rice cakes on top.
    5. Enjoy this refreshing Korean dessert!
