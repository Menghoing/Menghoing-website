  • Kimbap

    Preparation Time: 30 minutes

    Ingredients:

    • Sushi rice
    • Nori (seaweed sheets)
    • Pickled radish
    • Cucumber
    • Carrot
    • Egg
    • Sesame oil
    • Sesame seeds
    • Soy sauce

    Recipe:

    1. Cook the sushi rice and season it with sesame oil and a pinch of salt.
    2. Julienne the cucumber, carrot, and omelet (egg).
    3. Place a bamboo sushi rolling mat on a clean surface and put a sheet of plastic wrap on top.
    4. Place a sheet of nori on the plastic wrap with the shiny side down.
    5. Spread a thin layer of sushi rice on the nori, leaving a small border at the top.
    6. Layer the pickled radish, cucumber, carrot, and egg on the rice.
    7. Roll the gimbap tightly using the mat, squeezing gently as you roll.
    8. Seal the roll by moistening the exposed edge of the nori with water.
    9. Slice the gimbap into bite-sized pieces and serve with soy sauce.

  • Kimchi Stew

    Preparation Time: 45 minutes

    Ingredients:

    • Kimchi
    • Pork belly
    • Tofu
    • Green onions
    • Onion
    • Garlic
    • Ginger
    • Red pepper flakes
    • Soy sauce

    Recipe:

    1. Sauté pork belly, garlic, and ginger in a pot until fragrant.
    2. Add chopped kimchi, tofu, and onions to the pot and stir-fry for a few minutes.
    3. Add water, soy sauce, and red pepper flakes to the pot and simmer until the stew is flavorful and the ingredients are tender.
    4. Serve hot, garnished with green onions.

  • Bibimbap

    Preparation Time: 25 minutes

    Ingredients:

    • Steamed rice
    • Assorted vegetables
    • Beef
    • Egg
    • Gochujang (red chili pepper paste)

    Recipe:

    1. Sauté the beef and vegetables separately.
    2. Place steamed rice in a bowl and arrange the beef and vegetables on top.
    3. Fry an egg and place it on the center of the dish.
    4. Serve with gochujang and mix everything together before eating.

  • Pajeon

    Preparation Time: 20 minutes

    Ingredients:

    • All-purpose flour
    • Rice flour
    • Eggs
    • Water
    • Green onions
    • Zucchini
    • Carrots
    • Shiitake mushrooms
    • Soy sauce

    Recipe:

    1. Mix all-purpose flour, rice flour, eggs, and water to create a pancake-like batter.
    2. Add chopped green onions, zucchini, carrots, and shiitake mushrooms to the batter.
    3. Pour a ladle of the batter into a hot, oiled pan and cook until both sides are golden brown.
    4. Serve hot with soy sauce for dipping.

  • Tteokbokki

    Preparation Time: 30 minutes

    Ingredients:

    • Rice cakes (tteok)
    • Gochujang (red chili pepper paste)
    • Fish cakes
    • Scallions
    • Cabbage
    • Sugar
    • Soy sauce

    Recipe:

    1. Boil the rice cakes until they are soft.
    2. In a separate pot, make a sauce by mixing gochujang, sugar, and soy sauce.
    3. Add the sauce to the rice cakes and simmer until the sauce thickens.
    4. Add fish cakes, scallions, and cabbage to the pot and cook until everything is tender.
    5. Serve hot.

  • Jjajangmyeon

    Preparation Time: 45 minutes

    Ingredients:

    • Noodles
    • Pork or beef
    • Onion
    • Zucchini
    • Potato
    • Black bean paste (chunjang)
    • Cucumber

    Recipe:

    1. Boil the noodles and drain.
    2. In a separate pan, stir-fry the black bean paste, meat, and vegetables.
    3. Add water and let it simmer until the sauce thickens.
    4. Pour the sauce over the noodles and serve.

  • Samgye-tang

    Preparation Time: 2 hours

    Ingredients:

    • Whole chicken
    • Garlic
    • Sticky rice
    • Ginseng
    • Jujubes (Korean red dates)
    • Ginkgo nuts

    Recipe:

    1. Stuff the chicken with sticky rice, garlic, and ginseng.
    2. Simmer the stuffed chicken with jujubes until the meat is tender and the broth is rich.
    3. Serve hot, garnished with ginkgo nuts.

  • Kimchi

    Preparation Time: 1 hour

    Ingredients:

    • Napa cabbage
    • Daikon radish
    • Garlic
    • Ginger
    • Red pepper flakes
    • Fish sauce
    • Salt
    • Sugar

    Recipe:

    1. Cut the napa cabbage and daikon radish into bite-sized pieces.
    2. Prepare a paste with garlic, ginger, red pepper flakes, fish sauce, salt, and sugar.
    3. Coat the cabbage and radish with the paste, making sure they're well-mixed.
    4. Ferment the mixture for a few days or until it reaches the desired level of fermentation.
    5. Refrigerate and serve as a side dish with meals.
